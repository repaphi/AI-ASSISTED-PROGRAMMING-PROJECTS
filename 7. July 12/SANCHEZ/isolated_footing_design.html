S-02 — Design Theory

An isolated (pad) footing supports a single column and spreads its load over a large enough soil area that the soil doesn't fail or settle too much. NSCP footing design generally proceeds through the steps below.

(a) Soil classification — context, not a direct formula

Atterberg limits tell us how "clayey" or "plastic" the soil is:

Plasticity Index, PI = LL − PL

A high PI (say, above 20) suggests a compressible clay that is more sensitive to moisture, meaning settlement can matter as much as bearing capacity. Moisture content and Atterberg limits do not plug directly into the bearing-capacity formula — they classify the soil and sanity-check the qa value from the geotechnical report.

(b) Size the footing for service (unfactored) loads

We use actual, not factored, loads because soil pressure is a serviceability check, not a strength check:

P = PD + PL   →   A_req = P / q_net_allow

where q_net_allow already has the Factor of Safety baked in (qa = q_ultimate / FS), minus the weight of soil/footing displaced:

q_net_allow = qa − γ_conc × Df

(c) Check strength (factored loads) for the concrete itself

Once the plan size is fixed, factor the loads (NSCP 203 load combination) to get the soil pressure used for concrete design:

Pu = 1.2·PD + 1.6·PL   →   qu = Pu / A_footing

(d) Two-way (punching) shear check

The column tries to "punch" through the footing like a stamp through paper. NSCP checks shear on a critical perimeter located d/2 from the column face (d = effective depth):

bo = perimeter of critical section   →   Vc = 0.33·λ·√f'c·bo·d

This is usually the governing (most critical) check for isolated footings.

(e) One-way (beam) shear check

The footing also cantilevers off each column face like a wide beam. Critical section is at distance d from the column face:

Vc = 0.17·λ·√f'c·b·d

(f) Flexure (bending) design

Bending moment is taken at the face of the column (cantilever action), then solved like a normal RC beam section for required steel:

Mu = qu·b·(L_cant)² / 2   →   Rn = Mu /(φ·b·d²)   →   ρ, As

(g) Minimum steel & bar spacing

Footings need at least the shrinkage/temperature steel ratio (0.0018 for grade 415 steel, NSCP 407.6.1) even if flexure needs less.
